IEEE SecCPS 2026

1st Workshop on Cybersecurity of Next-Generation Networked Cyber Physical Systems

Cybersecurity is increasingly being recognised as a fundamental property of modern Cyber-Physical Systems (CPS). The rise of interconnectivity in CPS domains, such as the Internet of Things (IoT), smart grids, intelligent transport systems, and Industrial Control Systems (ICS) has resulted in improved efficiency to support a range of advanced use cases. However, this same interconnectedness exposes CPS to significant cyber threats that can directly disrupt physical operations, compromise human safety, and cause large-scale economic or environmental damage. Cybersecurity, therefore, is not a peripheral concern but a foundational requirement. Robust security frameworks ensure confidentiality, integrity, availability, and resilience within CPS, enabling them to operate reliably under adversarial conditions. Consequently, advancing CPS research and deployment demands an equally strong focus on adaptive, multi-layered cybersecurity strategies that safeguard both digital assets and the physical world they govern. We invite contributions that addresses digital resilience and cybersecurity challenges in evolving and future CPS domains. We will seek technical, empirical, and theoretical contributions that propose advancements in modelling, measurement, and analysis of defensive and offensive security topics within the context of CPS including but not limited to intrusion detection, privacy, encryption, network security, and organisational resilience. Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:

  • Anomaly/intrusion detection of cyberattacks in networked CPS
  • Artificial Intelligence for Digital Resilience in CPS
  • Secure CPS Network Communications
  • Privacy of CPS Networks and Data
  • Measurement of novel attack vectors targeting CPS
  • Security of federated digital twins and interconnected CPS
  • Zero-trust in CPS
  • Sustainable security for CPS
  • Human-centred and usable security aspects of CPS
  • Security of industrial wireless communications
  • Orchestration of CPS security using SDN and programmable networking
